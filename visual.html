Cafe Wall Illusion

The Café Wall illusion is a visual phenomenon where parallel lines appear to be sloped or curved due to the placement and pattern of adjacent colors or shapes.

If you click on the image, the colors will be changed to colors with less contrast, revealing that the lines are actually straight.


Penrose Stairs

The Penrose Stairs, also known as the "Impossible Staircase," is an optical illusion depicting a staircase that appears to ascend or descend endlessly in a loop. The steps are arranged in such a way that the viewer's brain interprets them as a logical structure, even though it is geometrically inconsistent.






Checker Shadow

This is an illusion that highlights the brain's interpretation of light and shadow. The illusion consists of a checkerboard pattern with alternating light and dark squares placed under a shadow cast by a cylinder, with 2 squares marked with A and B. The shadow covers part of the checkerboard as well as Square B. Despite the reality that Square A and Square B are the same color, we assume one is darker because it is under a shadow.



In the second iamge, the gray rectangle that connects between the 2 squares, A and B shows that both are clearly the same. This shows how we also use our enviroment, like lighting to determine the color of many things.
